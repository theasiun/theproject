Wall Street Journalist Wendy Bounds discusses the seriousness of the effects of violence in movies and media with Youtube Programming Director Marshall Crook. They discuss all the potential factors and how their effects. In one of their conclusions, they both agree that it is much more beneficial if a guardian or parent watches or participates in the movie or video game with the child to ensure that the child can understand what is really going on.

Dr. Sylvia Gearing discusses the effects of violent movies and games on young children and teens. She reminds that children and teens are still in stages of development and their brain is still unfolding. External influences and themes influence their future actions and thoughts. Parents must be aware that their are risk factors in watching these movies like mental illnesses or unhealthy ideas and thinking. Parents must set the guidelines in a caring and compassionate way in order for their children to be willing to compromise.

Reasearch has been done for the last 50 years, continuous exposure to violence do increase rates of violent thoughts and behaviors. However, there is not enough evidence to prove there is a direct correlation. It depends greatly on a variety of factors like characteristics. Everyone even adults and elderly are at risk as well. The most important thing to remember is that parent sneed to pay attention to what their children are watching, what their children's specific needs are, and who/where their children watch these types of content.
